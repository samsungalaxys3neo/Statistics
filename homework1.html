What is statistics, and why can it be useful for cybersecurity?

Due: 09 / 10 / 2025

Statistics transforms raw data into structured knowledge. It allows analysts to uncover trends, test hypotheses, and build predictive models from apparently random information.

In cybersecurity, statistics helps define what “normal” looks like — making it possible to detect outliers: unusual traffic, login spikes, or suspicious patterns.

By calculating averages, variances, and deviations, a system can raise alerts whenever new data significantly deviates from expected behavior. That’s how intrusion detection works in practice.
